## B) PRACTICAL INFORMATION (実用情報)

---

### B1. International Driving Permit (IDP / 国際運転免許証)

**Is an IDP legally required?** YES. Under Indonesian traffic law, all foreign drivers must carry BOTH:
1. Their national/home country driving license (原付免許 or 普通自動二輪免許 for Japanese)
2. A valid International Driving Permit (IDP / 国際運転免許証)

**IDP Category for motorbikes:** Category A is required for motorcycles/scooters. Category B is for cars. Your IDP must specifically include the motorcycle category. If your home country license does not cover motorcycles, your IDP will not cover motorcycles either.

**For Japanese tourists (日本人観光客):** Japan issues IDPs through the Japan Automobile Federation (JAF). You need a valid 普通自動二輪免許 (regular motorcycle license) or 原付免許 (moped license, 50cc and under only -- this will NOT cover Indonesian 110-125cc scooters). You specifically need at least a 普通自動二輪免許 (ordinary motorcycle license) to legally ride 110cc+ scooters in Indonesia.

**Can tourists legally ride motorbikes?** Technically YES, with proper IDP + national license with motorcycle category. In practice, many tourists ride without proper documentation. This is illegal and risky.

**Source:** [Indonesia IDP Info](https://internationaldrivingpermit.org/country/indonesia/), [Bali.com IDP Guide](https://bali.com/bali/travel-guide/transportation-guide/international-driving-license-for-bali/), [Wikipedia - Driving License Indonesia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Driving_license_in_Indonesia)

### B2. Insurance Situation (保険)

**Are tourists covered by rental insurance?** Generally NO. Rental shops in Indonesia typically do not provide insurance. The renter assumes full responsibility for vehicle damage or loss during the rental period.

**Travel insurance and motorbikes:** CRITICAL -- Many standard travel insurance policies EXCLUDE motorbike riding or only cover it if:
1. You hold a valid license (IDP + national license with motorcycle category)
2. You were wearing a helmet at the time of any accident
3. You were following local traffic laws

**Without proper IDP:** Your travel insurance will almost certainly REFUSE to cover any motorbike accident injuries or damage. This is a very serious financial risk -- tourists in Indonesia are charged 150% or more above local rates for hospital care.

**Recommended insurance:** LUMA travel insurance covers motorbike injuries for both driver and passenger, subject to wearing a helmet and following traffic laws. Check your specific policy before riding.

**Source:** [LUMA Insurance](https://www.lumahealth.com/travel-insurance/indonesia/), [Be Protected Indonesia](https://www.beprotectedindonesia.com/motorbike-insurance), [Backindo](https://www.backindo.com/riding-motorbike-indonesia/)

### B3. Police Stops & Fines (警察の取り締まり・罰金)

**What happens if police stop you without IDP?**
- Police conduct regular traffic stops (razia)
- You must show: vehicle registration (STNK, provided by rental shop), your national license, and your IDP
- Missing ANY of these typically results in a fine

**Typical fine amounts:**
- Missing IDP/license: Rp 300,000 - 500,000 per missing document
- Under Indonesian traffic law: driving without a license can result in fines up to Rp 1,000,000 (approximately US$67) or theoretically up to 4 months imprisonment
- In practice, on-the-spot "fines" (unofficial) may be negotiated lower, but this is corruption and not recommended to participate in

**Important:** Always carry the STNK (vehicle registration certificate) that the rental shop gives you, along with your IDP and national license. Keep photocopies of your passport separately.

**Source:** [IDP Requirements](https://internationaldrivingpermit.org/country/indonesia/), [Bali.com](https://bali.com/bali/travel-guide/transportation-guide/international-driving-license-for-bali/)

### B4. Fuel (ガソリン)

**Fuel type for scooters:** Pertalite (RON 90) -- this is the standard fuel used by most motorbikes in Indonesia.

**Price per liter (as of late 2025 / early 2026):**
- Pertalite (RON 90): Rp 10,000/liter (government-supervised price, consistent nationwide)
- Pertamax (RON 92, premium): approximately Rp 12,950/liter
- Solar (diesel): not applicable for motorbikes

**Fuel efficiency of common rental bikes:**
- Honda Beat 110cc: approximately 50-60 km/liter
- Honda Vario 125cc: approximately 45-55 km/liter
- Yamaha NMAX 155cc: approximately 40-45 km/liter
- Full tank (Honda Beat ~3.7 liters) = approximately 185-220 km range
- Full tank (Vario 125 ~5.5 liters) = approximately 250-300 km range

**Cost example:** A day trip Malang to Batu and back (~40 km round trip) would use less than 1 liter = ~Rp 10,000 in fuel.

**Petrol stations (SPBU):** Pertamina stations are found throughout Malang city and along major routes. On rural roads (e.g., toward southern beaches), stations become less frequent. In remote areas, roadside vendors sell fuel in recycled bottles (eceran) at a premium (typically Rp 12,000-15,000/liter).

**Source:** [Daihatsu Indonesia](https://daihatsu.co.id/en/tips-and-event/tips-sahabat/detail-content/harga-terbaru-spbu-pertamina-dan-swasta-maret-2025/), [AutoIni](https://www.autoini.com/fuel-price/indonesia-fuel-prices-november-2025/)

### B6. Traffic Conditions in Malang (マランの交通事情)

**General traffic:**
- Malang city center is compact and has moderate traffic
- Rush hours (07:00-09:00 and 16:00-18:00) can be congested, especially on Jl. Kawi, Jl. Ijen, and around the alun-alun area
- One-way streets exist in the city center -- notably around Jl. Basuki Rahmat and the commercial district
- In Indonesian cities, one-way rules generally apply to cars but motorbikes sometimes navigate them more freely (though this is technically illegal)

**Driving on the left:** Indonesia drives on the LEFT side of the road (same as Japan, UK). Japanese tourists will find this familiar.

**Indonesian traffic behavior:**
- Traffic is unpredictable and instinct-based rather than rule-based
- Drivers may suddenly turn, pull out, or U-turn without warning
- Horn usage is constant and expected -- use it when overtaking to signal your presence
- Right-of-way is negotiable, not fixed
- Wrong-way traffic is common on short stretches
- Drive slowly and defensively; speed is more dangerous than patience

**Source:** [Expat.or.id](https://www.expat.or.id/info/guidelinesforindonesianmotorbikedrivers.html), [Backindo](https://www.backindo.com/riding-motorbike-indonesia/)

## C) ROUTES & DAY TRIPS BY MOTORBIKE (バイクでの日帰りルート)

### C1. Malang to Batu (マラン → バトゥ)

**Distance:** 17-20 km (depending on route)
**Time:** 30-45 minutes (normal traffic)
**Road condition:** GOOD. Paved road throughout. Gentle uphill gradient as Batu is at higher elevation (~800m vs Malang ~450m). Well-maintained asphalt. The route passes through suburban areas and gradually becomes more scenic with mountain views.
**Scenic?** YES -- especially the latter half as you climb toward Batu. Views of rice paddies and mountains.
**Difficulty:** EASY. Suitable for beginners.
**Fuel needed:** Less than 1 liter round trip (~Rp 10,000)
**Attractions along the way:** Museum Angkut, Jawa Timur Park 2, BNS (Batu Night Spectacular)
**Recommendation:** An excellent first ride to gain confidence. The road is wide, well-paved, and not too steep.

**Source:** [ViaMichelin](https://www.viamichelin.com/web/Routes/Route-Batu-65317-Malang-Indonesia-to-Malang-65171-Malang-Indonesia), [Rome2Rio](https://www.rome2rio.com/s/Malang/Batu)

### C2. Malang to Candi Singosari (マラン → チャンディ・シンゴサリ)

**Distance:** 12 km north of Malang city center
**Time:** 20-30 minutes
**Road condition:** GOOD. The temple is located 500m off the main Malang-Surabaya road. Well-paved main road; short detour to the temple on local road.
**Difficulty:** VERY EASY. The easiest temple ride from Malang. Flat terrain, simple navigation.
**Recommendation:** Perfect for a quick morning ride. Combine with Candi Jago / Candi Kidal for a temple circuit.

**Source:** [Lonely Planet](https://www.lonelyplanet.com/indonesia/east-java/attractions/candi-singosari/a/poi-sig/1219504/1319463), [TravelFish](https://www.travelfish.org/sight_profile/indonesia/java/east_java/malang/1450)

### C3. Malang to Tumpang Temples (Candi Jago & Candi Kidal) (マラン → チャンディ・ジャゴ & キダル)

**Distance:** Candi Jago is 22 km from Malang (near Tumpang market). Candi Kidal is in Rejokidal village, Tumpang District.
**Time:** 30-40 minutes to Tumpang area
**Road condition:** GOOD. Paved roads. The route goes through small towns and village roads. Navigation is straightforward with Google Maps.
**Difficulty:** EASY. Manageable for anyone comfortable riding in Indonesian traffic.
**Recommended circuit:** Malang -> Candi Singosari (12km north) -> Candi Jago (Tumpang, 22km east) -> Candi Kidal (nearby) -> return to Malang. Total: approximately 60-70 km, 3-4 hours with visits.

**Source:** [Lonely Planet](https://www.lonelyplanet.com/indonesia/east-java/attractions/candi-kidal/a/poi-sig/1219519/1319463), [Keliling Nusantara](https://kelilingnusantara.com/tour/malang-temples-tour/)

### C4. Malang to Coban Rondo Waterfall (マラン → コバン・ロンド滝)

**Distance:** 14-30 km (depending on starting point in Malang; the waterfall is near Pujon)
**Time:** 45-60 minutes
**Road condition:** MOSTLY GOOD but with some challenges. The route to Pujon is mostly asphalt, but the final approach involves winding mountain roads with some rocky sections. The waterfall is at 1,135m elevation on the slope of Mount Banyak, so expect steep inclines.
**Difficulty:** MODERATE. The winding mountain road requires confidence on a motorbike. Not recommended for complete beginners.
**Scenic?** YES -- beautiful mountain scenery, rice terraces, and cool highland air.
**Safety note:** Use a vehicle robust enough for hilly terrain. A Honda Vario 125cc or larger is recommended over a small Beat for the steeper sections.

**Source:** [Idetrips](https://idetrips.com/coban-rondo-waterfall/), [TravelSetu](https://travelsetu.com/guide/coban-rondo-waterfall-tourism/how-to-reach-coban-rondo-waterfall)

### C5. Malang to Mount Bromo (マラン → ブロモ山) -- IMPORTANT SAFETY WARNINGS

**Distance:** Approximately 50-70 km (depending on route)
**Time:** 2.5-3 hours by scooter
**Road condition:** VARIABLE TO DANGEROUS.

**CAN YOU DO IT BY MOTORBIKE?** Technically yes, but with MAJOR caveats:

**DANGER WARNING -- AUTOMATIC SCOOTERS (Beat, Vario, Scoopy, etc.):**
Official warning banners have been posted by the Probolinggo Police Traffic Unit, the local Transportation Department, AND the Bromo Tengger Semeru National Park Authority advising visitors NOT to ride automatic scooters (motor matic) on the steep climbs and descents. The reason: on sharp descents, brake systems on automatic scooters overheat and lose function, leading to accidents.

**Route challenges:**
1. The main road from Malang involves steep inclines
2. Inside the national park, paved road stops and you must cross the "Sea of Sand" (Lautan Pasir) -- fine, grey volcanic sand that is extremely difficult to navigate on a scooter
3. The sand stretches approximately 5 km (3 miles)
4. If going for sunrise, conditions include darkness, sand, narrow winding roads, and jeep traffic
5. It is very easy to take a wrong turn from Malang to Bromo and end up on impassable tracks

**Recommended approach:**
- If you have motorbike experience: rent a DIRT BIKE from MOTO East Java (Honda CRF 150 handles the sand and slopes well)
- The route from Probolinggo (north side) is FAR EASIER than from Malang
- For most tourists: take an organized jeep tour (see Section E for cost comparison)
- Never attempt Bromo by scooter at night / pre-dawn

**National Park entrance fee:** Rp 255,000 (as of late 2024/2025 for foreign tourists)

**Source:** [Expat Life Indonesia](https://expatlifeindonesia.com/tourists-advised-against-riding-scooters-when-visiting-bromo/), [Mad or Nomad](https://www.madornomad.com/the-mount-bromo-motorcycle-guide/), [TripAdvisor](https://www.tripadvisor.co.nz/ShowTopic-g6643743-i31701-k12132584-Riding_scooter_from_Malang-Bromo_Tengger_Semeru_National_Park_East_Java_Java.html)

### C6. Malang to Southern Beaches (Balekambang etc.) (マラン → 南海岸ビーチ)

**Distance:** Approximately 65-70 km to Balekambang Beach
**Time:** 2 - 2.5 hours
**Road condition:** MIXED. The main road is mostly paved, but the final 2 km before Balekambang Beach has poor road conditions -- broken, rough, uneven surface described as "20-25 minutes of really rough up and down off-roading" on a motorbike. Slippery in wet conditions.
**Difficulty:** MODERATE TO HARD (due to final stretch). The long distance and rough final approach make this a tiring ride.
**Fuel:** Fill up before leaving Malang; petrol stations become sparse heading south. Round trip ~140 km = approximately 3 liters = ~Rp 30,000 in fuel.
**Recommendation:** Doable on a scooter if you are an experienced rider and accept the rough final stretch. Take it slow on the last section. A Honda Vario 125cc or larger is better than a Beat for this distance.

**Source:** [Uncharted Backpack](https://unchartedbackpack.com/destinations/malang/balekambang-beach/), [TripAdvisor](https://www.tripadvisor.com/Attraction_Review-g297710-d4809821-Reviews-Balekambang_Beach-Malang_East_Java_Java.html)

### C7. Malang to Tumpak Sewu Waterfall (マラン → トゥンパク・スウ滝)

**Distance:** Approximately 70 km
**Time:** 2.5 - 3 hours
**Road condition:** Generally GOOD. The main highway is well-maintained but essentially a one-lane road (not a freeway). The route goes through Dampit and Pronojiwo. The road gets busy between Turen and Malang.
**Difficulty:** MODERATE. The distance is long, and navigation requires attention. Avoid Google Maps shortcuts that may lead to rough, unfinished roads -- stick to the main road even if Google shows a shorter route.
**Warning:** The RIDE to Tumpak Sewu is manageable; the HIKE at the waterfall itself is the dangerous part (slippery bamboo steps, river crossings, especially in rainy season November-April).
**Fuel:** Fill up in Malang; limited stations along the route.

**Source:** [Chris and Wren's World](https://chrisandwrensworld.com/how-to-get-to-tumpak-sewu-waterfall/), [Lonely Planet](https://www.lonelyplanet.com/articles/guide-to-tumpak-sewu-indonesia), [TripAdvisor](https://www.tripadvisor.com/ShowTopic-g294228-i9251-k13848488-Driving_Scooter_Motorbike_to_Tumpak_Sewu_from_Malang-Java.html)

## D) SAFETY (安全情報)

---

### D1. Risk Level & Common Hazards

**Risk level:** Moderate to high for inexperienced riders. Indonesia has one of the highest road fatality rates in Southeast Asia. Motorbike riders are the most vulnerable road users.

**Common hazards:**
1. **Other drivers:** Sudden turns without signaling, wrong-way driving, unpredictable behavior
2. **Road quality:** Potholes (especially after rain), unfinished/broken road surfaces, loose gravel on rural roads
3. **Animals:** Stray dogs, chickens, goats -- especially on village roads and rural routes
4. **Night riding:** Very dangerous. Poor street lighting outside city center, other vehicles without lights, animals on road. AVOID whenever possible.
5. **Rain:** Roads become extremely slippery when wet. Indonesian rain is sudden and intense. If caught in heavy rain, pull over and wait.
6. **Sand/gravel:** On mountain roads and the Bromo sand sea -- scooter tires have no grip on loose surfaces
7. **Trucks and buses:** Large vehicles dominate inter-city roads and drive aggressively

### D2. Recommended Safety Gear

- **Helmet:** ALWAYS wear one. Rental helmets are often low quality -- consider buying a decent one (available from Rp 100,000-300,000 at motorbike shops in Malang)
- **Closed-toe shoes:** Essential. Never ride in sandals or flip-flops
- **Long pants:** Jeans or similar -- protects against road rash
- **Light jacket with long sleeves:** Additional skin protection
- **Sunglasses or clear glasses:** Protects eyes from dust, insects, and debris
- **Rain gear:** Most rentals include a raincoat
- **For off-road/Bromo:** Full protective gear (goggles, gloves, boots, knee/elbow protectors) -- available from MOTO East Java for Rp 50,000

### D3. Emergency Procedures if Accident Occurs

1. **Emergency numbers:**
   - General emergency: 112
   - Ambulance: 118 or 119
   - Police: 110
2. **DO NOT move seriously injured people** unless in immediate danger
3. **Contact your travel insurance** hotline immediately
4. **Take photos** of the scene, your injuries, and any vehicles involved
5. **Get a police report** (needed for insurance claims)
6. **Contact your rental company** -- their number should be saved in your phone
7. **Go to the nearest hospital** (see D4 below)

### D4. Hospitals Near Common Routes

**Main Hospital in Malang:**
- **RSUD Dr. Saiful Anwar (RSSA)**
  - Address: Jl. Jaksa Agung Suprapto No.2, Malang, Jawa Timur 65111
  - Phone: (0341) 362101
  - Class A Regional General Hospital (largest in Malang)
  - Has Emergency Department (IGD)
  - Owned by East Java Provincial Government

**Other hospitals in Malang:**

**For Batu area:** RS Baptis Batu

**Important note:** Hospital costs for foreigners in Indonesia can be 150-500% higher than for locals. Having valid travel insurance with motorbike coverage is essential.

## E) COMPARISON WITH OTHER TRANSPORT (他の交通手段との比較)

---

### E1. Motorbike vs Grab/Gojek for Temple Circuit

**Temple circuit:** Candi Singosari -> Candi Jago (Tumpang) -> Candi Kidal -> return to Malang (approximately 60-70 km total)

| Option | Estimated Cost | Pros | Cons |
|--------|---------------|------|------|
| **Rented motorbike** | Rp 70,000-85,000 (rental) + ~Rp 15,000 (fuel) + parking = **~Rp 90,000-110,000 total** | Complete freedom, stop anywhere, own schedule, can explore side roads | Must ride yourself, navigate, parking hassle |
| **Grab Car (multiple trips)** | ~Rp 40,000-60,000 per leg x 4 legs = **~Rp 160,000-240,000** | No riding needed, air conditioning | Must wait for drivers at each temple (may be hard in rural areas), less flexibility |
| **Grab Bike (ojek)** | ~Rp 20,000-35,000 per leg x 4 legs = **~Rp 80,000-140,000** | Cheap, no riding needed | No storage for bags, uncomfortable for long distance, finding drivers at rural temples is difficult |
| **Private driver (full day)** | **~Rp 400,000-600,000** | Most comfortable, door-to-door, local knowledge | Most expensive |

**Verdict:** Rented motorbike is the BEST option for the temple circuit if you are comfortable riding. It offers maximum flexibility at the lowest cost. Grab/Gojek availability becomes unreliable at rural temple sites.

### E2. Motorbike vs Organized Tour for Bromo

| Option | Estimated Cost | Pros | Cons |
|--------|---------------|------|------|
| **Rented scooter (NOT recommended)** | Rp 70,000-85,000 (rental) + Rp 15,000 (fuel) + Rp 255,000 (park entry) = **~Rp 340,000-355,000** | Cheapest option | DANGEROUS on sand sea, brake failure risk on descents, banned by authorities for matic bikes, no sunrise viewpoint access in dark |

| **Shared jeep tour from Malang** | **~Rp 650,000/person** | Standard tourist option, comfortable, sunrise viewpoint included, hotel pickup | Shared with strangers, fixed schedule, no freedom |
| **Private jeep tour from Malang** | **~Rp 1,200,000-1,250,000** (per jeep, 4-6 people) | Private, flexible timing, comfortable | Expensive for solo travelers |

**Verdict for most tourists:** The shared or private jeep tour is the safest and most practical way to visit Bromo from Malang. ### E3. When is Motorbike the BEST Option? (バイクが最適な場面)

**BEST for motorbike:**
- Temple circuit (Singosari, Jago, Kidal) -- maximum flexibility at lowest cost
- Malang to Batu day trip -- easy ride, beautiful scenery
- City exploration in Malang -- quick, cheap, no waiting for drivers
- Coban Rondo waterfall -- if you have riding experience
- Multiple short trips over several days -- rental cost is fixed per day regardless of trips

**AVOID motorbike (use alternatives instead):**
- Mount Bromo (use jeep or guided dirt bike tour)
- Southern beaches in rainy season (slippery roads)
- Night riding anywhere
- If you have ZERO motorbike experience -- Indonesia is NOT the place to learn
- If you do not have valid IDP -- legal and insurance risk
- Long distances in heavy rain
- If traveling with heavy luggage
